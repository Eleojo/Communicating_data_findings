  • it will be more readable putting the duration_sec variable in minutes.

Dataset Structure¶

The dataset consists of 183412 rows and 16 columns. It has only three qualitative variables which are member_gender,time_of_day,bike_share_for_all_trip,user_type.

The main feature(s) of interest in this dataset¶

  • I am most interested in time spent using the service and what category of users use the service most.This will help Ford go-bike to customize their services or dig deeper to find ways of involving a wider category of people.
  • I will also like to find out what is the most famous destination for the users of this service.

Features in the dataset that will help support investigation into feature(s) of interest¶

  • member_gender

def plot_hist(data,size,var,log_plot=False):
    if log_plot:
        plt.figure(figsize=[8, 5]);
        bins=10**np.arange(np.log10(data['duration_min'].min()),np.log10(data['duration_min'].max())+size,size)
        plt.hist(data=data,x=var,bins=bins);
        plt.xscale('log');

    else:
        plt.figure(figsize=[8, 5]);
        bins=np.arange(data[var].min(),data[var].max()+size,size)
        plt.hist(data=data,x=var,bins=bins);

Discussion on the distribution(s) of variable(s) of interest.¶

The duration_sec variable was in seconds and for easy understanding it was converted to minutes hence i changed the name to duration_min. Initially after plotting it showed a skewness to the right with a long tail. I converted the scale and bins to log to so i could see the true distribution and i saw that this variable is actually unimodal with it's peak around 100mins.

To investigate what time of day had more engagement by users i had to extract the AM or PM from the start_time variable and store in a new variable(time_of_day). I used the start_time instead of the end_time because if a person starts using the bike in the afternoon or evening it is most likely he will stop in the evening or afternoon also, but if i use the end_time i might not really capture what i want as someone can begin in AM and end in PM.

Unusual distributions and operations performed on the data to adjust and change the form of the data.¶

I created a qualitative variable of age from the user_age variable to help me view and interprete the relationships better. I dropped missing data from the dataset and the member_gender,end_station_id and member_birth_year columns were affected. 197 instances in the end_station_id which amounts to 0.1% of the data and 8265 instances on the member_gender, member_birth_year which is about 4.5% of the data.
